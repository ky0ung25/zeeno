✓ Do Use Fraunces italic (300 Light) for emotional emphasis within bold headlines — it creates contrast and warmth without losing hierarchy. Reserve it for the last 1–2 words of a heading.
✗ Don't Mix Fraunces italic into body copy or use it at small sizes — the optical axis is tuned for display use. At 14px or below it becomes illegible and loses its character.
✓ Do Use Plus Jakarta Sans 300 (Light) for all body copy. The low weight keeps long-form text airy and approachable, matching Zeeno's clean clinical tone.
✗ Don't Use font-weight 400+ for body paragraphs — it makes the page feel heavy and clinical in the wrong way. Reserve 500 and 600 for labels, badges, and navigation only.
02

Color System

Orange Family — Primary Brand Palette
#E8763A
Orange
--zn-orange
Primary brand color. CTA buttons, active states, key highlights, badge backgrounds, hover accents, border-left on pull quotes. Use sparingly — it commands attention.
#F5A265
Orange Light
--zn-orange-light
Hover states on orange buttons, gradient mid-stops, lifestyle section backgrounds, decorative glow elements. Never use as a standalone text color.
#FDE8D4
Orange Pale
--zn-orange-pale
Card borders with orange context, float card borders, subscription step dot backgrounds, hover fills on secondary elements. Good for warm tinting large areas.
#FEF4EC
Orange X-Pale
--zn-orange-xpale
Active variant card fill, pull quote background, selected state fills. The lightest warm tint — use for large selected or highlighted regions.
Blue Family — Secondary / Scientific Credibility
#1A5C96
Ocean
--zn-ocean
Deep scientific authority. Float card "Glare Recovery" stat, study section third accent, footer science link hover. Use for the highest-credibility moments.
#3A7EC8
Cerulean
--zn-cerulean
Secondary accent for science content, ghost button text/borders, ingredient color coding (blue family), eye diagram fills, "second stat" numbers. Complements orange without competing.
#6AA3DC
Cerulean Light
--zn-cerulean-light
Eye science diagram gradients, illustration fills. Rarely used as a UI color — primarily for decorative and visual elements.
#D4E8F8
Cerulean Pale
--zn-cerulean-pale
Float card borders (blue variant), ghost button borders, eye science diagram ring borders, subtle blue context fills.
#EEF5FC
Cerulean X-Pale
--zn-cerulean-xpale
Guarantee block background, trust/credibility zones. Creates visual differentiation from the orange-warm content areas without being cold.
Green Family — Confirmation & Savings
#2A9D72
Green
--zn-green
Savings badges, "Best Value" labels, verified purchase checkmarks, discount tags, ATC success state, subscription benefit text. Never use for primary branding.
#E6F7F1
Green Pale
--zn-green-pale
Savings badge fills, subscription benefit banner background, discount tag fills. Pairs with Green text for any "good deal" signaling.
Neutral Family — Text, Surfaces & Structure
#1A1A2E
Ink
--zn-text-dark
All primary headings, product title, nav logo, footer background, active tab backgrounds. The deepest tone — use only where maximum contrast is needed.
#4A4A6A
Slate
--zn-text-mid
All body copy, product subtitles, card descriptions, FAQ answers, tab labels when inactive, ingredient descriptions. The default text color for readable content.
#8888AA
Mist
--zn-text-light
Secondary labels, breadcrumbs, timestamps, footer column headers, helper text, "was price" strikethroughs, eyebrow label on dark backgrounds. Never for body copy.
#E8E8F0
Border
--zn-border
All dividers, card borders, table row separators, tab underlines, input borders, thumbnail borders in default state. The structural color — ubiquitous but invisible.
#FDF9F5
Off-White
--zn-off-white
Page background, purchase box fill, gallery main background, breadcrumb strip, alternate section backgrounds. Warmer than pure white — reduces eye strain.
#FEF5EA
Cream
--zn-cream
Warm alternate section backgrounds, lifestyle sections, subtle warmth zones. Noticeably warmer than Off-White — use to signal lifestyle/emotional content areas.
#FFFFFF
White
#fff
Card fills on off-white backgrounds, float card backgrounds, purchase box white panels, CTA button text on Orange. Always on a warm background — never on pure white.
✓ Do Pair Orange (#E8763A) with Ink (#1A1A2E) backgrounds for maximum contrast on dark sections (proof strip, footer). The warm-meets-deep combination is distinctly Zeeno.
✗ Don't Place Orange text on Cream or Off-White at body text sizes — the contrast ratio is too low for accessibility. Use Slate (#4A4A6A) for readable body text on warm backgrounds.
✓ Do Use the Green family exclusively for financial/confirmatory signals (savings, verified, success). This preserves its meaning and prevents color fatigue.
✗ Don't Introduce new brand colors outside this system. The orange-blue tension is intentional and clinically grounded. Adding purple, teal, or warm reds dilutes the brand identity.
