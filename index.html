The Science

Your eyes have built-in sunglasses.

Macular pigment—made from lutein + zeaxanthin—sits in the center of your retina. It absorbs blue light (400–500 nm, peak ~460 nm), acting like a natural yellow filter. Most adults don't get enough from diet alone to keep pace with modern screen habits.
